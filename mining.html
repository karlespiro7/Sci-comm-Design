The Mining
Life Cycle

From finding materials in the ground to restoring the land — these are the four phases of every mining cycle, explained through the journey of your smartphone.

📱

Why your smartphone?

A single modern smartphone contains over 60 elements — including lithium, cobalt, copper, and rare earth elements. Each of these elements require mining. Following these materials in your phone helps us understand how mining works.

1
Phase 01 · Exploration
Finding What's Underground

Before a single rock is moved, years of scientific discovery determine whether there are valuable materials present in the ground. They use tools like surveys, sampling, and drilling to understand what is below the surface and if it can be safely dug up.

📱
Smartphone Connection

Lithium for your phone's battery is often found in brine lakes in South America. Geologists test these areas to estimate how much lithium is available before mining begins.

⚖️
Regulatory Highlight

Companies must gain permits before exploration begins. They also have to assess the environmental impacts and communicate their plans with local and Indigenous communities.

2
Phase 02 · Development
Preparing the Site

Once a resource is confirmed to be reasonable, the development phase begins — often lasting 5–15 years. This includes building roads, facilities, and systems needed to safely get the materials to process them.

📱
Smartphone Connection

Copper (used in phone wiring) needs large processing areas that are built on-site before mining operations begin.

⚖️
Regulatory Highlight

A full Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) and mine plan must be approved before construction. These assess water use, tailings management, biodiversity impacts, and social effects. Nearby communities need to approve before anything is finalised.

3
Phase 03 · Extraction
Mining the Materials

This is the operational phase. Materials are removed from the ground and then get processed. Depending on the location, mining can occur at the surface or underground. Operations can run continuously for 20–50+ years.

📱
Smartphone Connection

The cobalt in your phone's battery comes with other metals. Large amounts of rock are processed to obtain small amounts of usable material.

⚖️
Regulatory Highlight

Mines must hold a valid mining lease and align with monitoring requirements — including water quality, air emissions, and noise levels. Regulators can come and check in to see how it is going and how it is keeping up with the community. Annual reports are typically mandatory.

4
Phase 04 · Reclamation
Restoring the Land

When mining is complete, companies need to restore the land. This includes reshaping the landscape, replanting vegetation, treating contaminated water, and monitoring the site for years or decades. Done well, reclaimed sites can become habitats or productive farmland.

📱
Smartphone Connection

Technology companies like Apple and Samsung are constantly tracking where materials come from to make sure that they are responsible and keeping up the environmental standards.

⚖️
Regulatory Highlight

Mining companies have to provide financial promises to make sure that the land is restored, even once the operations end. Monitoring after completion can last 10–30 years.
